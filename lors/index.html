Letters of Recommendation

← Home Teaching & Advising

I’m happy to support my students however I can, including by writing letters of recommendation. My goal is to write a letter that is both genuine and effective in helping you reach your goals.

Academic Letters

Please give me at least three weeks’ advance notice for most academic opportunities. For Ph.D. applications, please give me at least six weeks’ notice before your first deadline.

Please provide:

  1. A list of what you are applying to and the deadlines.
  2. Your current CV or résumé.
  3. A reminder of the courses or projects you completed with me.
  4. What you hope the letter will emphasize.

Ph.D. Applications

Letters for doctoral programs focus heavily on research potential. I can normally write these letters only when I have directly supervised or evaluated substantial original research.

Please also provide:

  • A draft of your statement of purpose.
  • Your writing sample or other relevant research.
  • A list of your other recommenders and their roles.

Non-Academic Letters

For professional opportunities outside academia, please provide at least two weeks’ notice, along with the position descriptions, deadlines, your résumé, and any points you would like emphasized.
